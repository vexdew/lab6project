MIT App Inventor - Multi-Screen App Development

This classwork introduced the basics of MIT App Inventor, a drag-and-drop app development platform. Using its intuitive Blocks Editor, I developed a multi-screen app showcasing user-friendly navigation and task management features.

Key Topics Covered:

  • Understanding the visual programming paradigm of MIT App Inventor.
  • Creating multi-screen navigation and managing screen transitions.
  • Designing and implementing logic blocks for app functionality.

App Features:

  • Multi-screen functionality with seamless transitions between pages.
  • Task creation, listing, and deletion capabilities for basic to-do list management.
  • Responsive layout tested across multiple Android devices.

Challenges Faced:

  • Encountered difficulties in building blocks logic for to-do list functionality, which caused prolonged debugging.
  • Managed to resolve issues with iterative testing and adjustments in the Blocks Editor.

Reflection:

This project highlighted the simplicity and accessibility of MIT App Inventor for beginner-friendly app development. Compared to Android Studio, I found MIT App Inventor much simpler to use due to its codeless approach. However, its limitations in handling complex functionalities made me appreciate the flexibility and power of traditional coding environments. Overall, I enjoyed the process and learned valuable lessons about logical thinking and iterative development.
